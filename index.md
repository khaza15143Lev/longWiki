## Concepts
- [[built-in-curling]] — Permanent curvature locked into slabs during construction from temperature/moisture differentials
- [[corner-loading]] — Traffic load at slab corner creating highest tensile stresses
- [[illi-slab]] — University of Illinois finite element program for jointed concrete pavement analysis
- [[construction-practices]] — Paving methods, curing, saw-cutting, and quality control influencing cracking risk
- [[curling-warping]] — Slab deformation from temperature (curling) and moisture (warping) gradients
- [[delamination-cracking]] — Horizontal separation at dowel depth distinct from vertical cracking
- [[dowel-bar-misalignment]] — Dowels outside tolerances reducing load transfer and inducing stress concentrations
- [[dowel-bars]] — Round steel bars for load transfer at transverse joints
- [[dry-climate-cracking]] — Longitudinal cracking in arid regions amplified by soil voiding
- [[drying-shrinkage]] — Volume reduction from moisture loss creating surface tensile stresses
- [[early-age-cracking]] — Cracking within days/weeks of placement before significant traffic loading
- [[environmental-loading]] — Stresses from temperature gradients, moisture differentials, and subgrade restraint
- [[expansive-soils]] — High-swelling subgrade causing differential settlement
- [[faulting]] — Vertical displacement at joints; major serviceability loss contributor
- [[frost-heave]] — Upward displacement from ice lens formation in cold climates
- [[jslab]] — Finite element program for jointed concrete pavement analysis
- [[late-afternoon-paving]] — Paving practice to allow concrete to set during cooler nighttime conditions
- [[load-transfer]] — Ability of joints to transfer traffic loads between slabs
- [[longitudinal-cracking]] — Cracking parallel to traffic direction in PCCP
- [[longitudinal-joint-separation]] — Opening at longitudinal construction joints
- [[longitudinal-cracking-case-studies]] — Inventory of 20+ field projects (1935–2024) documenting longitudinal cracking across the US
- [[loss-of-support]] — Reduction of subgrade/subbase contact from voiding, erosion, or settlement
- [[ltpp-sps-2]] — Long-Term Pavement Performance Specific Pavement Studies; nationwide field study on structural factors
- [[maturity-method]] — Non-destructive technique for estimating early-age concrete strength
- [[model-calibration]] — Adjusting mechanistic-empirical design parameters to match local conditions
- [[nighttime-cracking]] — Cracking initiated during nighttime negative temperature gradients
- [[nonlinear-temperature-gradient]] — Nonlinear temperature profiles increasing top-slab stresses 80% over linear assumptions
- [[pavement-me]] — AASHTO mechanistic-empirical pavement design software
- [[pca-fatigue-equation]] — PCA empirical fatigue equation relating stress ratio to load repetitions
- [[premature-cracking]] — Longitudinal cracking within first few years of service life
- [[probabilistic-crack-model]] — Statistical model for predicting crack confinement in saw-cuts
- [[saw-cut-depth]] — Critical depth for creating controlled weakened planes
- [[shoulder-design]] — Shoulder configuration influencing edge support and cracking potential
- [[skewed-joints]] — Transverse joints at angle to longitudinal direction increasing corner stress
- [[slab-curl]] — Upward/downward curvature from temperature or moisture differentials
- [[slab-geometry]] — Length, width, thickness determining stress distribution and cracking orientation
- [[soil-voiding]] — Void formation from slab curl depressurizing fine-grained soils
- [[stabilized-base]] — Subbase layer with bound materials (HMA, lean concrete) for uniform support
- [[subbase-erosion]] — Loss of subbase material from water infiltration causing void formation
- [[subsealing]] — Rehabilitation technique for void filling beneath slabs
- [[summer-paving]] — Hot-month paving creating conditions for positive built-in curling
- [[temperature-gradient]] — Temperature difference between top and bottom surfaces driving curling
- [[tie-bars]] — Deformed steel bars across longitudinal joints preventing separation
- [[tied-concrete-shoulder]] — Structurally connected concrete shoulder via tie bars
- [[top-down-cracking]] — Cracking initiated at slab surface and propagating downward
- [[vibrator-trails]] — Linear defects from over-consolidation during paving
- [[weakened-plane]] — Deliberately created zone controlling crack location
- [[widened-slab]] — Slabs wider than 12 ft; reduce transverse cracking but increase longitudinal cracking
