# Construction Practices

Paving methods, curing procedures, saw-cutting timing and depth, and quality control measures that influence longitudinal cracking risk in JPCP.

## Key Factors

- **Saw-cut depth**: Must exceed D/3 for 12-ft slabs to create effective weakened plane
- **Vibrator frequency**: Over-vibration creates vibrator trails with reduced air content
- **Paving timing**: Late-afternoon paving reduces built-in curling risk
- **Curing**: Controlled moisture retention reduces differential drying shrinkage
- **Joint spacing**: Shorter joint spacing reduces cumulative shrinkage stress

## Key Findings

- [[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]] identified construction practices as the primary cause of early-age cracking, not slab width
- [[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]] documented shallow saw-cuts and vibrator trails as root causes of premature longitudinal cracking in Colorado
- [[lim-tayabji-2005-early-age-longitudinal-cracking]] found first-night cracking risk driven by paving width and timing
