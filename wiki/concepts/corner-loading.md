# Corner Loading

Traffic load applied at the corner of a concrete slab, where stress concentrations are highest due to combined effects of load position, slab curl, and joint geometry.

## Mechanism

Corner loading creates higher tensile stresses at the slab top than midslab edge loading. When combined with upward slab curl (negative temperature gradient), the unsupported corner condition amplifies stresses significantly.

## Key Findings

- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] found corner loading creates stresses approximately 40% higher than midslab edge loading with positive temperature gradient and no shrinkage gradient
- [[dere-asgari-sotelino-archer-2006-skewed-jpcp]] demonstrated skewed joints further concentrate corner stresses at acute angles, increasing cracking risk
