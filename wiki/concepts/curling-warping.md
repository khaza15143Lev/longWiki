# Curling and Warping

Slab deformation caused by temperature gradients (curling) and moisture gradients (warping) through the slab thickness.

## Mechanism

Curling results from temperature differentials; warping results from moisture differentials. Combined curling and warping stresses can exceed concrete tensile strength, initiating top-down cracking.

## Key Findings

- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] found sites with higher degrees of curling and warping measurements exhibited significantly more longitudinal cracks
- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] demonstrated differential drying shrinkage causes permanent upward curl at slab corners
- [[quan-et-al-2014-built-in-curling]] quantified positive built-in curling locked in during summer paving
