# Dry Climate Cracking

Longitudinal cracking mechanism specific to arid and semi-arid regions where soil voiding from slab curl depressurization is amplified by low moisture content.

## Mechanism

In dry climates, fine-grained subgrades lack the moisture needed to maintain soil structure when subjected to negative pressure from slab curl. The resulting void formation is more rapid and extensive than in humid regions, leading to accelerated loss of support and cracking.

## Key Findings

- [[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]] proposed the soil voiding mechanism from slab curl depressurization, noting dry zones are particularly susceptible; validated through LTPP SPS-2 data
