# Early-Age Cracking

Longitudinal cracking that occurs within days or weeks of concrete placement, before significant traffic loading.

## Mechanism

Driven by early-age environmental gradients (temperature, moisture), drying shrinkage, and construction practices. The first night after paving is the most critical period for crack initiation.

## Key Findings

- [[lim-tayabji-2005-early-age-longitudinal-cracking]] found first-night cracking risk is driven by paving width; widths <24 ft show minimal risk
- [[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]] identified construction practices as the primary cause of early-age cracking, not slab width
- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] observed 37 of 74 slabs cracked under environmental influences before any traffic loading
