# Environmental Loading

Stresses induced in concrete pavements by temperature gradients, moisture differentials, and subgrade restraint, independent of traffic loading.

## Mechanism

Environmental loading includes thermal expansion/contraction, slab curling from temperature gradients, warping from moisture differentials, and subgrade restraint. Combined environmental and traffic loads exceed concrete tensile strength more frequently than traffic loading alone.

## Key Findings

- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] found 37 of 74 slabs cracked under environmental influences before any traffic loading
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] identified high degrees of curling/warping as one of five primary causes of longitudinal cracking
- [[quan-et-al-2014-built-in-curling]] quantified built-in curling stress from summer paving conditions
