# Expansive Soils

Subgrade soils with high swelling potential that undergo volume change with moisture variation, contributing to differential settlement and longitudinal cracking in concrete pavements.

## Characteristics

Per [[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]] (Region 1, Colorado):
- High plasticity index (PI = 30-39)
- Lower R-value (resistance to lateral movement)
- Fraction passing No. 200 sieve close to 100% (highly plastic clayey natural soils) (p. 6)
- Very sensitive to moisture change; substantial movement with moisture penetration

## Distress Pattern

Cracking with differential settlement:
- Cracks wander between center of lane and wheel paths (Fig. 2, p. 4)
- Differential settlement visible across cracks
- Subgrade fill materials may be under-compacted
- Frost action amplifies the problem when water penetrates through cracks

## Mitigation

- Accurate recognition and prediction of volume change potential before construction ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]], p. 12)
- Proper treatment of expansive soils prior to construction
- Adequate subgrade compaction
- Crack sealing to prevent water infiltration into subgrade
