# Faulting

Vertical displacement difference between adjacent concrete slabs at a transverse joint or crack. A major contributor to loss of serviceability in jointed concrete pavements.

## Causes

Per [[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]]:
- Loss of subgrade support (voids beneath slab)
- Deterioration of load transfer system (dowels, aggregate interlock)
- Combination of both mechanisms

## Impact

- "Faulting of the slabs is the largest contributor to the loss of serviceability of a PCC pavement" ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], p. 1)
- Creates rough ride, increases dynamic loading on vehicles
- Accelerates joint deterioration through increased impact stresses

## Diagnosis

Nondestructive testing can distinguish between the two causes:
- Low modulus of subgrade reaction (k < 200 pci) → void/subgrade loss
- Low modulus of dowel-concrete interaction (G < 200,000 pci) → load transfer failure

Correct diagnosis is critical because rehabilitation methods differ: subsealing for voids vs. load transfer reestablishment for dowel deterioration ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], pp. 1-2, p. 7).
