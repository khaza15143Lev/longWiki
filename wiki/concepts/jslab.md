# JSLAB

A finite element computer program developed by Tayabji and Colley at [[construction-technology-laboratory]] (a division of PCA) for analyzing jointed concrete pavement systems.

## Capabilities

Per [[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]]:
1. Stresses and deflections in up to nine slabs with longitudinal and transverse joints
2. Two-layer systems (bonded or unbonded)
3. Load transfer modeled with dowels, aggregate interlock, or keys
4. Full or partial subgrade contact analysis; thermal gradient curling stresses (independent or combined with traffic loads)
5. Joints with nonuniformly spaced load transfer devices

## Validation

- Validated against Westergaard's exact equations for interior, edge, and corner loading
- Good agreement demonstrated in parametric comparisons ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], pp. 2-3, Fig. 1)

## Applications

Used to develop evaluation charts (Figs. 10-11) that enable practitioners to back-calculate modulus of subgrade reaction (k) and modulus of dowel-concrete interaction (G) from nondestructive testing measurements.
