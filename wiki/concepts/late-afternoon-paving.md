# Late-Afternoon Paving

Construction practice of placing concrete during late afternoon hours to allow the slab to set during cooler nighttime conditions, reducing built-in curling risk.

## Mechanism

Concrete placed in late afternoon sets during the night, when temperature gradients are less severe and the slab contracts uniformly. This minimizes positive built-in curling (upward edge curl) that occurs when concrete sets under hot daytime conditions with steep surface-to-bottom temperature differentials.

## Key Findings

- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] observed a site constructed in late afternoon, where concrete set during nighttime and led to less built-in curling, only slightly exhibited longitudinal cracks
- [[quan-et-al-2014-built-in-curling]] quantified that summer daytime paving creates maximum positive built-in curling, increasing both level and frequency of tensile stress on top of PCC slab
- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] found nighttime negative gradients increase top-surface tensile stresses, but daytime positive gradients during setting are more detrimental for long-term cracking

## Design Implications

Recommended practice for JPCP construction, particularly in hot climates and for widened slabs. Should be specified in construction contracts alongside curing requirements.
