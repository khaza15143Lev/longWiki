# Load Transfer

The ability of a joint in a concrete pavement to transfer traffic loads from one slab to an adjacent slab. Essential for reducing stresses and deflections under wheel loads.

## Mechanisms

Per [[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]]:
- **Dowel bars** — Steel bars across transverse joints providing mechanical load transfer
- **Aggregate interlock** — Interlocking of coarse aggregate particles across cracked or jointed faces
- **Keyed joints** — Mechanical interlock formed by shaped joint geometry

## Quantification

- **Modulus of dowel-concrete interaction (G)** — Stiffness parameter characterizing the load transfer system; typical assumed value: 1.5 x 10^6 pci ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], p. 5)
- **Joint efficiency** — Ratio of deflection on unloaded slab (Point B) to deflection on loaded slab (Point A); measures load transfer effectiveness ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], pp. 3-4)
- **Limiting threshold**: G < 200,000 pci indicates poor joint system requiring rehabilitation ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], p. 7)

## Factors Affecting Load Transfer

Parametric study found that dowel diameter, dowel modulus of elasticity, and joint width have minimal effect on corner deflection and joint efficiency. The dominant factor is the modulus of dowel-concrete interaction ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], pp. 4-6).

## Rehabilitation

Loss of load transfer (low G) requires reestablishment of load transfer through dowel retrofit or joint replacement; subsealing will not restore load transfer ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], p. 7).
