# Loss of Support

Reduction or elimination of subgrade/subbase contact beneath a concrete slab due to voiding, erosion, or settlement.

## Mechanism

When support is lost, the slab acts as a cantilever over the unsupported area, creating high tensile stresses at the top surface. Loss of support can occur from soil voiding, subbase erosion, or differential settlement.

## Key Findings

- [[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]] found subbase erosion creates catastrophic loss of support leading to longitudinal cracking
- [[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]] proposed soil voiding mechanism from slab curl depressurization in dry climates
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] identified inadequate subgrade support as one of five primary causes of longitudinal cracking
