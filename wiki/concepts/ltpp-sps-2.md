# LTPP SPS-2

Long-Term Pavement Performance (LTPP) Specific Pavement Studies (SPS-2), a nationwide field study examining the structural factors contributing to concrete pavement performance, including slab width, base type, and joint configuration.

## Key Findings

- **Widened slab performance**: SPS-2 data was used by [[michael-darter]] and others to validate 14-ft slab design, which CDOT adopted in 1996 ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]]).
- **Soil voiding mechanism**: [[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]] analyzed SPS-2 sections to identify slab curl depressurization of fine-grained soils as a distinct cracking mechanism in dry climates.
- **Mixed results on width**: SPS-2 data contributed to the widened slab debate; some sections showed increased longitudinal cracking with wider slabs, while others performed adequately.

## Design Implications

SPS-2 data informed mechanistic-empirical design calibration and supported the use of widened slabs (14 ft) under proper construction quality control. Results remain contested in the context of the [[widened-slab]] debate.
