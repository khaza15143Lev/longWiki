# Nighttime Cracking

Longitudinal cracking that initiates during nighttime hours when negative temperature gradients (cooler top surface) create extreme top-surface tensile stresses.

## Mechanism

During nighttime, the slab top cools rapidly while the bottom remains warm from daytime heat storage. This negative gradient causes upward slab curl, creating tensile stresses at the top surface that can exceed early-age concrete tensile strength.

## Key Findings

- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] found highest concrete tensile stresses developed at top of slab under extreme negative temperature gradients during night; first night is critical period
- [[lim-tayabji-2005-early-age-longitudinal-cracking]] identified first-night cracking risk driven by paving width; widths <24 ft show minimal risk
