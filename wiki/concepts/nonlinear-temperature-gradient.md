# Nonlinear Temperature Gradient

Temperature profiles through concrete slab thickness that deviate from linear assumptions, creating higher tensile stresses at the slab surface.

## Mechanism

AASHTO MEPDG simplifies temperature distribution to a linear difference between top and bottom surfaces. Actual profiles are nonlinear, with steeper gradients near the surface. This nonlinearity increases top-slab tensile stresses by approximately 80% compared to linear gradient assumptions.

## Key Findings

- [[dere-asgari-sotelino-archer-2006-skewed-jpcp]] demonstrated nonlinear gradients increase top-slab stresses 80% over linear models in 3D FEA
- [[quan-et-al-2014-built-in-curling]] showed current AASHTO MEPDG simplification underestimates built-in curling stress because it assumes linear temperature distribution
- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] found nighttime negative gradients are particularly nonlinear, creating extreme top-surface tensile conditions

## Design Implications

FEA models using linear temperature gradients significantly underestimate cracking risk. Accurate pavement design requires nonlinear gradient input, especially for skewed joints and widened slabs.
