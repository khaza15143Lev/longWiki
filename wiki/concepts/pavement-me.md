# AASHTOWare Pavement ME

Mechanistic-empirical pavement design software developed by AASHTO for predicting pavement performance under traffic and environmental loading.

## Limitations

- Does not predict longitudinal cracking for JPCP, indicating a gap in mechanistic-empirical design
- Simplifies built-in curling to linear temperature difference between top and bottom surfaces, underestimating nonlinear gradient effects
- Requires local calibration for accurate distress prediction; California calibration showed widened shoulders perform poorly in SP-2 sections

## Key Findings

- [[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]] calibrated Pavement ME for California conditions and found it cannot predict longitudinal cracking for JPCP; widened shoulders performed poorly in SP-2 test sections
- [[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]] identified the gap in Pavement ME's ability to model early-age construction-related distresses
