# Premature Cracking

Longitudinal cracking that occurs within the first few years of pavement service life, before design traffic loads are reached.

## Mechanism

Premature cracking is driven by a combination of construction practices, environmental gradients, and early-age material properties, rather than accumulated traffic fatigue.

## Key Findings

- [[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]] documented premature longitudinal cracking in Colorado Region 1 attributed to shallow saw-cuts and vibrator trails
- [[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]] identified construction practices as primary cause of premature cracking
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] found traffic volume correlates with cracking severity, but environmental factors initiate cracks pre-traffic
