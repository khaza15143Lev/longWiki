# Saw-Cut Depth

The depth of saw-cut joints in concrete pavements, critical for creating a [[weakened-plane]] that controls where cracking occurs. Insufficient depth causes uncontrolled cracking outside the joint.

## Specifications

Per [[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]] (CDOT):

| Slab Width | Required Saw-Cut Depth |
|---|---|
| 12-ft (standard) | D/3 (one-third of slab thickness) |
| 14-ft (wider) | 0.4D (40% of slab thickness) |

The 0.4D requirement for wider slabs mimics European specifications for concrete pavements wider than 12 feet ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]], p. 11).

## Field Findings

- Saw-cuts as shallow as 50% of required depth found on Colorado projects ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]], p. 11)
- At I-70 Deer Trail, saw-cuts measured 2-2.25 inches on 10.25-inch pavement (35% shallower than D/3) ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]], p. 10)
- Non-working shoulder joints (where saw-cut did not crack through) correlated with longitudinal cracking in travel lanes

## Quality Control

CDOT implemented new specification requiring engineers to measure saw-cut depth at intervals of 1 per 1/10 of a mile (528 feet) ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]], p. v, p. 12).
