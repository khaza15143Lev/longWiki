# Skewed Joints

Transverse joints in concrete pavements oriented at an angle to the longitudinal direction, rather than perpendicular to traffic.

## Mechanism

Skewed joints create asymmetric stress distributions at the joint corners. Under traffic loading, the acute-angle corner experiences higher tensile stresses than rectangular joints. Nonlinear temperature gradients exacerbate this effect, increasing longitudinal cracking potential.

## Key Findings

- [[dere-asgari-sotelino-archer-2006-skewed-jpcp]] demonstrated through 3D FEA that skewed joints concentrate stresses at acute corners, creating conditions for longitudinal cracking perpendicular to traffic
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] confirmed pavements with skewed joints are prone to longitudinal cracks compared to rectangular joints under all loading scenarios; recommended avoiding skewed joints for widened JPCP with high truck traffic

## Design Implications

Rectangular joints are preferred for widened JPCP, especially in high-traffic areas. When skewed joints are unavoidable (e.g., at intersections), additional reinforcement or reduced joint spacing may be required.
