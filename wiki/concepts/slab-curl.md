# Slab Curl

Upward or downward curvature of a concrete pavement slab caused by temperature or moisture differentials between top and bottom surfaces.

## Mechanism

Positive curl (edges up) occurs under negative temperature gradients (cooler top); negative curl (edges down) occurs under positive gradients (warmer top). Daily cycles cause reversible curl, while permanent built-in curl from construction setting conditions remains locked in.

## Key Findings

- [[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]] found slab curl depressurizes soil beneath lifted edges, creating voids in fine-grained soils
- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] observed slab corners permanently curled upward due to differential drying shrinkage, resulting in unsupported corner condition at all times
- [[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]] identified high negative temperature gradient (slabs curl up) combined with axle loads on adjacent slabs creates extremely high top tensile stresses
