# Subbase Erosion

Loss of subbase material due to water infiltration and pumping, leading to void formation and loss of support beneath concrete slabs.

## Mechanism

Water infiltrates through joints and cracks, carrying fine subbase particles out of the support layer. Stabilized subbases (HMA/PCC) can erode catastrophically when drainage is inadequate, creating voids that eliminate slab support and trigger longitudinal cracking independent of traffic loading.

## Key Findings

- [[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]] found loss of support from eroded stabilized subbases directly correlates with longitudinal cracking in areas with poor drainage
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] identified inadequate subgrade support as one of five primary causes of longitudinal cracking in widened JPCP

## Mitigation

- Proper drainage design (edge drains, permeable bases)
- Use of stabilized subbases with adequate erosion resistance
- Timely sealing of joints and cracks to prevent water infiltration
