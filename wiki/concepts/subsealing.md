# Subsealing

A rehabilitation technique that involves injecting grout or other material beneath a concrete slab to fill voids and restore subgrade support.

## Application

Per [[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]]:
- Used to correct loss of subgrade support (voids) identified by low modulus of subgrade reaction (k < 200 pci)
- Does NOT restore load transfer; separate treatment required for deteriorated dowels or aggregate interlock

## Decision Criteria

- Low k (poor subgrade) → subsealing is appropriate
- Low G (poor load transfer) → subsealing will not help; load transfer reestablishment needed
- Wrong repair scheme "wastes money and will not extend the serviceability of the pavement" ([[ozbeki-et-al-trr-1043]], p. 7)
