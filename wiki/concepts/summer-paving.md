# Summer Paving

Concrete pavement construction conducted during hot summer months, creating conditions favorable for positive built-in curling.

## Mechanism

Hot ambient temperatures and solar radiation create steep positive temperature gradients during concrete setting. The top surface sets at higher temperature than the bottom, locking in positive built-in curl (upward edge curl) that increases top-surface tensile stresses during subsequent cooler periods.

## Key Findings

- [[quan-et-al-2014-built-in-curling]] found summer daytime paving leads to maximum positive built-in curling, increasing both level and frequency of tensile stress on top of PCC slab
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] recommended late-afternoon paving to avoid excessive upward built-in curling
