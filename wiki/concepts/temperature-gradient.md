# Temperature Gradient

Temperature difference between the top and bottom surfaces of a concrete pavement slab, driving curling and warping stresses.

## Mechanism

Positive gradients (top warmer than bottom) cause downward curling; negative gradients (top cooler) cause upward curling. Nighttime negative gradients are particularly severe, creating high top-surface tensile stresses.

## Key Findings

- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] found highest concrete tensile stresses develop at top of slab under extreme negative temperature gradients (during night)
- [[quan-et-al-2014-built-in-curling]] demonstrated nonlinear temperature profiles through slab thickness increase top-slab tensile stresses by ~80% compared to linear assumptions
- [[dere-asgari-sotelino-archer-2006-skewed-jpcp]] incorporated nonlinear gradients in 3D FEA, showing critical role in skewed joint cracking
