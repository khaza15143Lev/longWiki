# Tie Bars

Deformed steel bars placed across longitudinal joints to hold adjacent slabs in contact and prevent joint separation.

## Mechanism

Tie bars do not provide load transfer; they maintain aggregate interlock at longitudinal joints by preventing separation. When tie bars fail or are undersized, joints open, aggregate interlock is lost, and longitudinal cracking accelerates.

## Key Findings

- [[chen-2014-longitudinal-joint-repairs]] documented tie bar failure mechanisms causing longitudinal joint separation; slot stitching and dowel retrofit are primary repair methods
- [[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]] found tied PCC shoulders outperform HMA shoulders because tie bars maintain slab-shoulder connection
