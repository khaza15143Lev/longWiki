# Tied Concrete Shoulder

A concrete shoulder structurally connected to the main pavement slab via tie bars, providing continuous load transfer and support at the longitudinal edge.

## Mechanism

Tied concrete shoulders act as an extension of the main slab, distributing traffic loads across a wider area and reducing edge stresses. Tie bars prevent separation at the longitudinal construction joint between the main slab and shoulder.

## Key Findings

- [[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]] found sites with tied PCC shoulders exhibited fewer longitudinal cracks than sites with HMA or granular shoulders; LTE of only 10% between shoulder and slab bases for untied HMA shoulders
- [[yang-et-al-2020-widened-concrete-pavements]] confirmed tied PCC shoulders outperformed HMA and granular shoulders even under higher traffic volumes; recommended tied PCC shoulders for widened JPCP with high truck traffic
- [[chen-2014-longitudinal-joint-repairs]] documented tie bar failure mechanisms and repair methods for longitudinal joint separation

## Design Implications

Tied concrete shoulders are preferred for widened JPCP, particularly in high-traffic applications. HMA and granular shoulders provide minimal load transfer benefit and do not reduce longitudinal cracking risk.
