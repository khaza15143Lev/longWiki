# Top-Down Cracking

A failure mechanism in concrete pavements where cracks initiate at the slab surface and propagate downward, contrary to traditional bottom-up fatigue models.

## Mechanism

Top-down longitudinal cracking initiates at the surface, typically 0.6–1.2 m from the longitudinal edge. The mechanism is driven by:
- **Environmental curling**: Differential temperature and moisture gradients cause the slab to curl upward at edges, creating tensile stresses at the top surface
- **Drying shrinkage**: Early-age moisture loss creates tensile stresses at the slab surface that exceed concrete tensile strength
- **Traffic interaction**: Once initiated, traffic loading accelerates downward propagation, though initiation is environmental

## Evidence

- [[heath-roesler-2000-top-down-cracking-FSHCC]] first documented top-down cracking as the dominant longitudinal cracking mode in JPCP
- [[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]] confirmed surface initiation 0.6–1.2 m from the edge in Iowa widened slabs
- [[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]] validated the mechanism through LTPP data and fracture mechanics modeling
