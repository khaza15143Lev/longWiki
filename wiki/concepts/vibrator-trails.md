# Vibrator Trails

Linear weakened planes in concrete pavement caused by malfunctioning or improperly adjusted paver vibrators. Appear as straight longitudinal cracks at a consistent distance from the pavement edge.

## Mechanism

- Vibrator frequency set too high OR prolonged vibration at low paver speed
- Over-consolidates concrete mix
- Causes non-uniform dispersion of aggregate particles
- Reduces air content in the vibrator path
- Creates a weakened plane where longitudinal cracking initiates

## Evidence

[[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]] documented:
- Cores from vibrator trails showed consistently lower air content than cores taken 18 inches away in lane center (pp. 7-8)
- Cracks along vibrator trails appeared straight and at consistent distance from pavement edge (Fig. 1, p. 4; Fig. 5, p. 7)
- Occurred even in areas where shoulder joint saw-cuts were at proper depth (p. 8)
- Split tensile strength testing inconclusive for strength difference (Table 1, p. 9)

## Prevention

CDOT specification requires paving contractors to equip machines with vibrator frequency monitoring devices ([[ardani-et-al-2003-colorado]], p. v, p. 12), following Iowa DOT's specification approach.
