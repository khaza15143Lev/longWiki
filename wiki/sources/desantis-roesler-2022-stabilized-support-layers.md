# Performance Evaluation of Stabilized Support Layers for Concrete Pavements

**Report** FHWA-ICT-22-003, Illinois Center for Transportation  
**Sponsor** Illinois Department of Transportation (R27-193-6), FHWA

## Overview

Evaluates erosion potential of stabilized subbases (HMA and PCC) under concrete pavements in Illinois through field surveys (FWD, MIRA, coring) and laboratory Hamburg Wheel-Tracking Device (HWTD) testing. Identifies subbase erosion as a cause of longitudinal cracking through loss of support.

## Key Claims

1. **Subbase erosion causes longitudinal cracking** — Loss of support from eroded stabilized subbases leads to severe distresses including pumping, faulting, corner breaks, and longitudinal cracking that spans multiple slabs ([[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]], pp. 161-164).

2. **HMA subbases performed well** — Field survey results showed HMA stabilized subbases did not lead to poor performance of concrete pavements across tested sections ([[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]], pp. 192-193).

3. **PCC subbase erosion in poor drainage** — Evidence of erosion in poor drainage locations (underpasses, sag vertical curves) for pavements with PCC stabilized subbase, though no faulting observed at transverse joints ([[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]], pp. 196-197).

4. **Bonded concrete overlay erosion-susceptible** — One site with bonded concrete inlay over existing composite pavement (HMA over PCC) was erosion-susceptible, contributing to severe failures along with heavy truck traffic, inadequate slab thickness, and longitudinal joints in outer wheel path ([[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]], pp. 193-196).

5. **CAM II cement content affects erosion** — Laboratory HWTD testing of Illinois CAM II (Cement Aggregate Mixture II) showed mixes with lower cement contents were more likely to experience erosion than mixes with higher cement contents ([[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]], pp. 178-179).

6. **HWTD recommended for erosion testing** — Hamburg Wheel-Tracking Device recommended as performance test for assessing erodibility of HMA or PCC stabilized subbase support layers. No changes recommended for HMA subbases; HWTD testing should be considered for PCC stabilized layers ([[desantis-roesler-2022-stabilized-support-layers]], pp. 199-200).
