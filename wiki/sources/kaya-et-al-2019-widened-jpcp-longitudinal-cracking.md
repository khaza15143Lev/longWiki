# Numerical Analysis of Longitudinal Cracking in Widened Jointed Plain Concrete Pavement Systems

**Journal** International Journal of Pavement Research and Technology 12(3), pp. 277-287  
**Sponsor** Iowa Department of Transportation, Iowa State University

## Overview

Numerical analysis of longitudinal cracking mechanism in widened JPCP using FEA. Identifies critical four-axle truck load configuration with axle loads near transverse edges as highest cracking scenario. Compares shoulder design alternatives for mitigation.

## Key Claims

1. **All longitudinal cracks are top-down** — Field investigations in Iowa confirmed all longitudinal cracks are top-down cracks starting mostly from transverse joints about 0.6-1.2m (2-4ft) away from widened slab edges ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 89-92).

2. **Critical loading scenario** — Four-axle truck with center of axle loads placed close to transverse edges identified as critical loading scenario. When axle loads on adjacent slabs, tensile stresses transferred to critical slab, resulting in very high tensile stress accumulation around top surface close to transverse edge ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 845-859).

3. **Negative temperature gradient amplifies** — High negative temperature gradient (slabs curl up) combined with axle loads on adjacent slabs transfers top tensile stresses to critical slabs, creating extremely high top tensile stresses around transverse joints ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 851-859).

4. **Tied PCC shoulders outperform HMA** — Sites with tied PCC shoulder exhibited fewer longitudinal cracks than sites with HMA shoulders. Tied PCC shoulder design performs better than HMA and granular shoulders for longitudinal crack potential ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 91-92, 912-914).

5. **Widened slabs increase cracking potential** — Higher top-to-bottom tensile stress ratio and top tensile stress observed with widened slabs (4.3m/14ft) with partial-depth tied PCC shoulder compared to regular slabs (3.7m/12ft) with full-depth tied PCC shoulder ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 866-873).

6. **HMA shoulders minimal effect** — HMA shoulder not tied to slabs, so no load transfer between slab and HMA shoulder; LTE of only 10% between shoulder and slab bases. Widened or regular slabs with HMA shoulders demonstrate similar behavior to no shoulder ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 883-892).

7. **Five mitigation recommendations**:
   - Longitudinal cracks mainly in traffic lane 0.3-0.6m (2-4ft) from slab edge
   - Shorter joint spacing reduces curling/warping and longitudinal cracking
   - Most cracks start from slab transverse joints
   - Proper dowel bar installation helps mitigate (restrains vertical deflection)
   - Tied PCC shoulder design outperforms HMA and granular shoulders ([[kaya-et-al-2019-widened-jpcp-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 896-914)
