# Early-Age and Premature Cracking in Jointed Plain Concrete Pavements: Literature Review

**Report** UCPRC-TM-2020-03, University of California Pavement Research Center  
**Sponsor** California Department of Transportation (65A0628), PPRC Project 4.74

## Overview

Literature review identifying factors leading to early-age and premature cracking in JPCP, with focus on California applications. Finds most cracking cases related to poor construction practices, with agreement on mechanisms but limited study of premature cracking specifically.

## Key Claims

1. **Poor construction practices primary cause** — In most reported cases, early-age and premature cracking related to poor construction practices: over-consolidation (low entrained air, segregation), poor dowel alignment (locked joints), multi-lane paving under different weather, paving on frozen base, non-uniform base thickness, built-in curling from hot weather paving, poor curing ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 1425-1431).

2. **Early-age cracking mechanism agreed** — Likelihood depends on interaction between tensile stresses developing after placement and concrete's early-strength gain. Tensile stresses attributable to hygrothermal actions as concrete dries and cools ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 1454-1458).

3. **17% slab replacements cracked by age 5** — California analysis showed about 17% of total individual slab replacements cracked by age 5. Statistical model predicts 50% cracking after 10 years for 0.7ft undoweled slabs on heavily trafficked roads, but worst 20% reach same condition in <5 years ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 637-647).

4. **Widened slabs debate unresolved** — Mixed results: SPS-2 and Louisiana studies show increased longitudinal cracking with widened slabs; Colorado and Wisconsin studies attribute cracking to construction issues, not slab width. Colorado found lower cracking in 14ft panels than 12-13ft panels ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 1403-1410).

5. **Three prevention practice categories**:
   - Mixture design: cement type/content, aggregate type, SCMs, admixtures
   - Pavement design: joint spacing, sawcut depth, bond breaker, old-new concrete connection
   - Construction: base conditioning, sawcut timing, proper curing, hot/cold weather practices ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 1462-1478)

6. **Premature cracking not well studied** — Unlike early-age cracking, premature cracking of JPCP has not been studied extensively. LTPP SPS-2 analysis shows premature cracking is problem in dry states like California ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 1490-1492).

7. **Early-age condition impacts long-term performance** — Widely recognized but very few studies focused on determining what that impact is. 2018 Mountain-Plains Consortium report showed increasing curing compound application rate improved roughness performance ([[mateos-harvey-2021-early-age-premature-cracking]], pp. 1433-1436).
