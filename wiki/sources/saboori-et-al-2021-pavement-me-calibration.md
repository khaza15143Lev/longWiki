# Pavement ME JPCP Transverse Cracking Model Calibration and Design Catalog Framework (Version 2.5.5)

**Report** UCPRC-RR-2020-02, University of California Pavement Research Center  
**Sponsor** California Department of Transportation (PPRC SPE 3.49)

## Overview

Calibrates Pavement ME transverse cracking model for California JPCP using ~4600 lane-miles of PaveM database data from 446 lane replacement projects (1947-2017). Reduces prediction bias from 13.3% to 0.039% and standard error from 23.03% to 5.69%.

## Key Claims

1. **Widened shoulders perform poorly** — JPCP with widened concrete shoulders (WRF) do not perform well; statistical model predicts performance similar to flexible shoulders. This is because widened shoulders used to be constructed in 14ft slabs, which are susceptible to longitudinal cracking ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4350-4353).

2. **Lean concrete base worst performer** — Among JPCP base types, lean concrete base (LCB) has poorest cracking performance; cement-treated base (CTB) and hot mix asphalt (HMA) have best ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4345-4346).

3. **Dowels don't impact cracking** — Neither presence nor absence of dowels in JPCP impacts its cracking performance ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4341-4342).

4. **Thicker/shorter slabs perform better** — JPCP with thicker and shorter slabs performs much better than thinner and longer slabs ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4336-4338).

5. **Tied shoulders outperform untied** — JPC pavements with untied flexible shoulders and no shoulders show more cracking than JPCPs with tied concrete shoulders ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4349-4351).

6. **Climate matters** — Worst performance in Inland Valley, High Mountain, South Mountain regions. Best in South Coast, Central Coast, High Desert, Desert regions ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4357-4360).

7. **National model overpredicts** — Pavement ME transverse cracking model overpredicts by ~13% nationally; bias is 19% for long slab pattern but only 3.3% for short slab pattern ([[saboori-et-al-2021-pavement-me-calibration]], pp. 4373-4381).
