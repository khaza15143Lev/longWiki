# Longitudinal Cracking at Transverse Joints Caused by Dowel Bars in Jointed Concrete Pavements

**Journal** KSCE Journal of Civil Engineering 17(2), pp. 395-402  
**Sponsor** National Research Foundation of Korea (NRF 2010-0007816), Korea Expressway Corporation

## Overview

Investigates longitudinal cracking at transverse joints in JCP through field observation and 3D FEA (ABAQUS). Identifies vertical translation of dowel bars from mid-depth as primary cause, with stress concentrations occurring when slabs curl under environmental loading before traffic opening.

## Key Claims

1. **Vertical dowel translation is primary cause** — Dowels installed above mid-depth cause localized transverse tensile stress concentrations at their location when slab curls up. Properly installed mid-depth dowels do not induce longitudinal cracking ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 395-396, 401).

2. **Cracks occur before traffic opening** — Field cracks observed before pavement opened to traffic, propagated through cement (not aggregates), indicating early-age environmental loading as primary cause ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 396).

3. **Stress concentration localized** — Transverse stress increment at vertically translated dowel is localized; does not affect whole stress distribution. Multiple translated dowels do not interact — each produces same stress increment independently ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 398-399).

4. **Center slabs more vulnerable** — Stress increment at translated dowel location increases as dowel moves from slab edge toward center. Translated dowels near slab center produce stress larger than maximum center stress, creating high cracking probability ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 397-398).

5. **Five factors increase cracking risk** — Transverse stresses increase with: (1) higher concrete elastic modulus, (2) higher thermal expansion coefficient, (3) higher foundation stiffness, (4) larger vertical temperature gradient, (5) greater vertical translation amount from mid-depth ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 399-400).

6. **Bond characteristic affects crack initiation** — When translated dowel is free to move: stress concentrated on slab surface and under dowel hole. When perfectly bonded: large stresses both on surface and above dowel hole. Crack initiation position depends on bond condition ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 400-401).

7. **Field validation** — 6 of 400 joints (1.5%) showed longitudinal cracking. Cores confirmed cracks at dowel locations with dowels installed far above mid-depth. Some cores showed horizontal delamination at dowel depth ([[seo-kim-2013-dowel-longitudinal-cracking]], pp. 396).

## Notable Quotes

- "To mitigate longitudinal cracking at the joint, dowel bars should be installed at the mid-depth of the slab and cares must be taken when installing dowel bars to prevent their vertical translation." (p. 401)
