# Analysis of Cracking in Jointed Plain Concrete Pavements

**Report** FHWA-HRT-16-073, Federal Highway Administration  
**Researchers** University of Cambridge, Department of Engineering

## Overview

Analyzes LTPP SPS-2 data to identify trends in longitudinal and transverse cracking. Proposes alternative mechanism for premature cracking: voiding in foundation soil due to localized plastic deformation from depressurization caused by slab curl, particularly problematic in dry regions with low cohesive strength soils.

## Key Claims

1. **Soil voiding mechanism** — Premature cracking explained by voiding in foundation soil due to localized plastic deformation. Depressurization of soil caused by slab curl creates voids, particularly problematic for soils with low cohesive strength in dry regions ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2093-2100).

2. **Two longitudinal crack patterns** — (1) Single long crack ~1m from pavement edge adjacent to shoulder; (2) Short crack near centerline of slab. Most longitudinal cracks initiate from slab transverse edges ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2074-2084).

3. **Transverse cracks initiate from shoulder edge** — Most transverse cracks initiate from slab longitudinal edge close to the shoulder ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2082-2084).

4. **Dry zones worse** — Cracks occur earlier and are more severe in dry climatic zones compared to wet zones ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2069-2071).

5. **Slab thickness and base type most sensitive** — Severity of longitudinal and transverse cracking sensitive to slab thickness and base type. Slab width and strength have less clear effect ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2062-2066).

6. **Edge pumping follows cracking** — In Arkansas (wet zone), edge pumping occurred as result of cracking rather than vice versa ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2088-2090).

7. **Slab curl + traffic + weak soil interaction** — Premature cracking mechanism involves interaction of slab curl, vehicle loading, and foundation soils with low cohesive strengths ([[xu-cebon-2017-lttp-cracking-analysis]], pp. 2054-2058).

## Notable Quotes

- "A plausible explanation for premature cracking in plain jointed concrete pavements is the occurrence of voiding in the foundation soil due to localized plastic deformation. This can occur because of depressurization of the soil caused by slab curl." (p. 2093-2100)
